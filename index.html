Frequently Asked Questions
Which fertilizer is suitable for coconut trees? +

For mature coconut trees, apply about 0.34 kg urea, 0.17 kg rock phosphate, and 0.34 kg muriate of potash per tree each year. Split the dose into two applications‚Äîonce before the monsoon and once after. Adding organic manure or compost improves soil health and yield.

How can farmers control banana stem borer? +

Regularly remove and destroy affected stems. Apply neem cake at the base of plants and set up pheromone traps to catch adult borers. For severe infestations, use recommended insecticides from the Krishi Bhavan or local agricultural office.

What are the government schemes available for farmers in Kerala? +

Kerala farmers can benefit from schemes like Subhiksha Keralam (for integrated farming), PM-KISAN (income support), and Kisan Credit Card (easy loans). The Agriculture Department also provides subsidies for seeds, irrigation, and farm machinery through Krishi Bhavans.

How can farmers get their soil tested in Kerala? +

Farmers can collect soil samples from their fields and submit them to the nearest Krishi Bhavan or district soil testing lab. The results include nutrient levels and fertilizer recommendations, helping improve crop yield and soil quality.

What crops grow well in the hilly regions of Kerala? +

Hilly areas like Wayanad and Idukki are ideal for plantation crops such as pepper, coffee, cardamom, tea, and banana. These crops thrive in well-drained soil and high rainfall conditions typical of the Western Ghats.

How can farmers improve the yield of pepper plants? +

Farmers can improve pepper yield by planting high-yielding varieties such as Panniyur-1 or Karimunda, providing proper support trees, and maintaining shade and drainage. Using compost or cow dung along with chemical fertilizers twice a year helps growth. Spraying Bordeaux mixture prevents quick wilt disease, and regular pruning keeps vines healthy.
